Copyright

What is Copyright

  1. Found in Title 17 of the U.S. Code
  2. Protection provided by law
  3. Protects the author of the original works

What Copyright isn't

  1. Patent: a Short term monopoly
  2. Trademark: Identifying a product or service in the marketplace

Availiable to

  1. Published and unpublished works
  2. Section 106 of 1976 Copyright Act

Copyright gives a exclusive right and ability to authorize

  1. Reproduction of work in copies or phonorecords
  2. Derivative works based upon the work
  3. Distribution of copies publicly by sale, transfer of ownership, rental, lease, or lending
  4. Performance of the work publicly
  5. Display of the work publicly

Copyright does not protects

  1. Ideas
  2. Facts
  3. Systems
  4. Methods of operation

When is the work protected?

  1. The moment it is created and fixed in a tangible form
  2. It is visible either directly or with the aid of a machine or device

Right of Regestration

  1. Registartion is voluntary
  2. Copyright exists from the moment the work is created
  3. To file a lawsuit from infringement,you will have to register

Copyright terms depends on many factors

  1. Individuals: the life of the author plus an additional 70 years
  2. Corporations: for contract works and certain other works,copyright protection lasts 95 years form first publication

Fair use doctrine

  1. is a guideline that determines how much a copyrighted item can be used legally without permission
  2. Section 107 of Title U.S. code contains exceptioin for reproduction of a particular work

Exceptions are

  1. Criticism, comment, news reportings, teaching scholarship, and reasearch
  2. Public domain items/works

Rules of thumb

  1. Limit the use of copies to single chapters
  2. Single articals of a journal issue
  3. Several charts, graphs, and illustration
  4. other Small Parts of work

Caveats

  1. There is no specific number of words, lines, or notes that may be taken without permision
  2. Acknowledging the source of the copyrighted material does not su stitute for obtaining permission

Bottom line

  1. Document your research at all times (bibliography and foot notes)
  2. if you publish copyrighted work on your own website or in another publication, get written permission
